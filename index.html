Aikido Plate

This is one of the first things I made in ceramics class. It is a plate with the Suenaka/Suenaga family crest in the center. Roy Suenaka cerated a style of Aikido called 'Suenaka Ha Tetsugaku Ho Aikido' I study this martial art, and made this as a house warming gift for one of my Shihans(Master Instructor). My makers mark is on the back as well as the japanese kanji for Aikido. The white is 'Cotton Tail' white, the crest in the center is glazed with 'Tuxedo' black both are Stroke&Coat brand.

niter container

This is a container I made to store some niter/saltpetre/potassium nitrate. It is glazed with a 'Poo Bear' glaze as a base, and 'Tuxedo' black glaze for the symbol, both are Stroke&Coat brand glaze.

charcoal container

This is not entirely my project. A friend made it but when it was fired some other unglazed piece exploded and got shraptnil stuck to it, so she gave it to me. I sanded and filed down the shraptnil and glazed over it with 'Tuxedo' black from Stroke&coat. Then I put the symbol for charcoal compound on the sides of it with 'Cotton Tail' White glaze also Stroke&coat brand. I intend on using this container to store charcoal compound in it.

Plant tray

This is a plate I made using a technique called mishima. I made it to collect the water that comes out of the botton of a pot with a christmas cactus in it. I formed the lip without a mold, I waited until it was leather hard then pinched and bent the edge. It's glazed with a 'Cotton Tail' white and 'Peri Twinkle' blue glazes from Stroke&Coat.

Aqua Tofana Container

This is a container I made to store some Aqua Tofana/Arsenic Trioxide. It is made with a coil technique and then covered up with some more clay. The lid had to be made twice because the first one didn't fit. The inside is glazed with a mixture of "Candy Apple' red and 'Tuxedo' black, the outside is just 'Tuxedo' black. the symbol on the lid is done with a gold paint pen from NEWELL, and then covered up with clear glue to seal it and pervent the gold from wairing off.

  • Tri Prima
  • In alchemy, "tria prima" refers to the three fundamental principles or "primes" - sulfur, mercury, and salt - believed to be the building blocks of all matter and the basis of alchemical transformation.

  • The Four Elements.
  • Air: In alchemy, air represents a life-giving force which we associate with the colours white & blue. Air to the alchemist represents the mind & soul of mankind. It also represents the breath of life. Air is also the sacred name of God, YHWH or Yahweh.
  • Fire: In alchemy, fire represents emotions such as passion, love, anger, and hate, which are sometimes referred to as “fiery” emotions. Aristotle labeled it as hot and dry, and it is represented by the colours red and orange, as well as the humor yellow bile. Additionally, fire is also seen as a more masculine symbol.
  • Earth: In alchemy, earth was believed to be primarily dry, and secondarily cold, (as per Aristotle). Beyond those classical attributes, the chemical substance salt, was associated with earth and its alchemical symbol was a downward-pointing triangle, bisected by a horizontal line.
  • Water: In alchemical thought, water represents the feminine principle, emotion, intuition, and the ability to transform and be transformed. It's symbolized by an inverted triangle, often associated with the colour blue.

Sitting Man

I had a the idea to make this figurine on a friday, I made it the following monday. I am honstly suprised I remembered it. The figurine is made to represent my self back when I was training; faceless, battered, injured, and broken, but still maintaining focus and form. There is a song "Eyes Without a Face" by Billy Idol, sums up how I was "living". The figureene has cuts and scars covering the body. It is sitting in seiza. The face has only eyes to represent the selflessness in which we were trained into. I used 'Ruby Slippers' red by Scroke&Coat for the cuts.
